30 DAYS TO BEGIN

According to Parkinson’s Law, work expands to fill the time allocated to it. The same task that takes eight hours will often take two when the constraint is tightened. A thirty-day goal is short enough to prevent diffusion, yet long enough to force structure, making it an ideal window for intentional progress.

I’m building this to help teens around me and anywhere else who want to start something meaningful but don’t know where to begin.
This website is about some "things" I made that I think anyone can do it in 30 days.

Short-term goals lower the pressure.
You’re not deciding your whole future, just the next few weeks.
That makes it easier to begin, easier to adjust, and easier to keep going.
Nothing here is perfect. It’s just a way to move from thinking to doing.

Time constraints force you to choose.
You can’t do everything, so you do the important thing.
That pressure isn’t a flaw. It’s what turns effort into direction.
