The average person types around 45 words per minute, which is a normal rate. Reaching 60 to 70 WPM is considered suitable for most work environments, while 80 WPM and above is often regarded as an advanced level. Touch typing boosts both productivity and speed, allowing you to focus on the task itself rather than the keys under your fingers. It’s an essential skill in almost any workspace today. You can practice using platforms like typingclub.com, monkeytype.com and typing.com, among many others. Best of all, touch typing is a skill that’s surprisingly easy to pick up in a short amount of time.

A 2025 study looking at over 120 research papers found that students who enjoy what they learn do better and learn faster. Positive feelings like fun and interest were linked to higher performance, showing that when learning is enjoyable, it becomes much easier and more effective. Source The easiest way to make learning fun is to actually make it feel like a game. Give yourself small goals, try to beat your own scores, or set little challenges—it makes practicing way less boring. Mix things up too, like using colorful notes, watching short videos, or practicing with different exercises. Doing it with friends or even just comparing progress with them can make it more interesting. And of course, the best trick is to learn things you care about—type your favorite lyrics, write a story, or mess around with stuff that actually excites you. It just makes everything click.

Honestly, the easiest way to manage your time is to actually see it. Break your day into chunks—like school, homework, breaks, and hobbies—so nothing sneaks up on you. Use a timer or phone alarm to stay on track, and tackle the important stuff first. You don’t have to plan every single hour of the day, and it’s okay if things don’t go perfectly—don’t stress yourself or push too hard, you’re not a machine. Just notice what didn’t work, avoid repeating it next time, and build small habits that make managing your time way easier than you’d expect.

Keeping track of what you’re doing is a MUST. It helps you see if you’re actually improving and teaches you a lot about yourself. When you record your goals or log your practice, you can notice real progress—and that’s super rewarding. You don’t need anything fancy; a notebook, an app, or even a simple checklist works. Tick off what you finish, note what’s tricky, and celebrate even the small wins. Tracking shows you what’s working, what’s slowing you down, and what to improve next. Plus, seeing how far you’ve come makes staying consistent way easier and way more satisfying.
